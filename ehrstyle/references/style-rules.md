# EHR House Style — §2 of the Notes for Contributors

Verbatim transcription of Section 2 of the *Economic History Review*
Notes for Contributors (Feb 2026 v1), restructured as rules + examples.
When in doubt on a case not covered here, EHR defers to *The Concise
Oxford Dictionary*, *The Oxford Writers' Dictionary*, and *Hart's Rules
for Compositors and Readers at the University Press, Oxford*.

General: "Authors are asked to bear in mind that their work should be
accessible to non-specialists. They should use clear language, write
impersonally, avoid specialized technical terms as far as possible, and
relegate lengthy discussions of method, or collections of data, if these
are needed at all, to appendices."

---

## 2.1 Numbers (figures)

- **1 through 9 in words**: *six women*, *seven years*.
- **10 and above in figures**: *88 feet*, *17 weeks*.
- **Exception — series**: when numbers form a series, all go in figures:
  *8 sheep, 12 horses, and 36 goats*.
- **Centuries**: *eighteenth century*, not *18th century*.
- **Decades**: *1930s*, not *1930's*.
- **All percentages in figures**: *8 per cent*. In text write *per cent*
  and *per annum*; in tables and footnotes use *p.a.* and *%*.
- **Large numbers in text**: *5.5 million*, not *5,500,000*, *5.5m*, or
  *five and a half million*.
- **Thousands**: use commas in numbers with four or more figures:
  *879*, but *3,602*, *15,827*.
- **Fractions**: hyphenate — *two-thirds*.
- **Sequences (including dates)**: avoid unnecessary repetition —
  *10–13*, *20–6*, *101–7*, *151–7*.

## 2.2 Years

- *1801–4* means from 1801 to 1804.
- *1801/2* means a calendar year (or part) crossing two adjacent years.
- *1852–72*, NOT *1852–1872*.

## 2.3 Dates

- In text: full form — *30 January 1938*.
- In footnotes (or endnotes): abbreviate months from August through
  February inclusive with full stops — *Aug.*, *Sept.*, *Oct.*, *Nov.*,
  *Dec.*, *Jan.*, *Feb.* (March–July unabbreviated.)

## 2.4 Equations

- Numbered sequentially throughout the article.
- Number in brackets at the end of the line where the equation appears.

## 2.5 Quotations

- **Single quotes** outer: `'…'`.
- **Double quotes** for a quotation within a quotation: `'… "…" …'`.
- **British conventions** on punctuation placement: the closing inverted
  comma PRECEDES a comma or full point.
- **Ellipsis**: three points (NOT four), with a letter space before and
  after — `( ... )`.
- **Block quotes**: quotations of 5+ lines or more than ~55 words should
  be broken off from the text and NOT enclosed in quote marks.

## 2.6 Italics and roman

- **Roman type** for commonly used abbreviations: *ibid.*, *et al.*,
  *i.e.*, *e.g.*
- **Italic** for:
  - Foreign words and phrases (e.g. *passim*, *Wirtschaftswunder*)
  - Titles of journals, newspapers, and books (e.g. *The Times*)

## 2.7 Oxford comma (mandatory)

- Insert a comma before *and* in a sequence of three or more items:
  - *red, white, and blue* — YES
  - *red, white and blue* — NO

## 2.8 Spelling (-ize / -ise)

- **-ize preferred** over -ise, but with the list of exceptions given in
  *The Oxford Writers' Dictionary* and *Hart's Rules*.
- **-yse, not -yze**: *analyse*, *catalyse*, *paralyse*.
- **Omit accents** in: *elite*, *regime*, *role*.
- **Specific words** — EHR prefers:
  - *acknowledgement* (not acknowledgment)
  - *despatch* (not dispatch)
  - *focused* (not focussed)
  - *enquiry* (general), but *inquiry* only when official
  - *judgement* (general), but *judgment* only in legal contexts

### Common -ize conversions required

| British -ise (AVOID) | EHR -ize (USE) |
|---|---|
| organise, organisation | organize, organization |
| recognise, recognisable | recognize, recognizable |
| emphasise | emphasize |
| summarise | summarize |
| formalise | formalize |
| synthesise | synthesize |
| characterise | characterize |
| theorise | theorize |
| institutionalise | institutionalize |
| generalise | generalize |
| industrialisation | industrialization |
| democratisation | democratization |
| mobilisation | mobilization |
| stabilisation | stabilization |
| radicalisation | radicalization |
| marginalise | marginalize |
| legitimise | legitimize |
| colonise | colonize |
| optimise | optimize |
| prioritise | prioritize |

### Words that keep -ise or -yse (do NOT convert)

*advise, analyse, advertise, catalyse, comprise, compromise, despise,
devise, exercise, expertise, franchise, improvise, likewise, merchandise,
otherwise, paralyse, practise* (verb), *promise, revise, supervise,
surprise, televise.*

## 2.9 Capitalisation

Rule: use lower case whenever possible.

### Keep capitals (use EHR's list):

- **Political/administrative divisions**: *Northern Ireland*, *West
  Beirut*, *South Carolina*, *East Anglia*, *the East End of London*,
  *the Highlands and Islands*.
- **Geographical features**: *the Bay of Bengal*, *Puget Sound*.
- **Specific buildings**: *Romsey Abbey*, *Faneuil Hall*.
- **Titles/ranks with a proper name**: *Harold, King of England*;
  *Thomas, Archbishop of Canterbury*; *John Smith, Mayor of Wandsworth*.
- **Religious denominations and sects**: *Protestant*, *Catholic*,
  *Quaker*, *Methodist*, *Plymouth Brethren*. (But lower-case: *puritan*,
  *nonconformist*.)
- **Parties**: *the Chinese Communist Party*, *the Labour government*,
  *the Liberal Democrats*, *the Whigs*.
- **Parliamentary bodies**: *the Commons*, *the Lords*, *the House*,
  *Parliament*. (But: *parliamentary*.)
- **Specific acts/bills**: *the Bubble Act of 1720*. (But: *the act* or
  *the bill* if not named.)
- **The Crown** (the institution). (But: *the state*.)
- **The City of London** (institution). *Bank of Nova Scotia* (when full
  title used).
- **Named eras/events**: *First World War* (NOT *World War I*),
  *Hundred Years War* (no apostrophe), *French Revolution*,
  *Irish Famine*, *Black Death*, *Blacks and Whites*.
- **Institutions in text**: *the Treasury*, *the Foreign Office*.
  (But lower-case: *city council*, *boards of guardians*.)
- **Currencies/rates**: *Bank Rate*, *French francs*,
  *Canadian dollars* (names of currencies of specific countries).
- **Ideological affiliations by named person**: *Keynesian*,
  *Thatcherite*. (But: *marxism*, *marxist*.)

### Use LOWER case (EHR's specific directives):

- **Geographical divisions where less specific**: *south Wales*,
  *north-western England*, *central and eastern Europe*.
- **Finance terms not tied to a proper name**: *income tax*,
  *estate duty*.
- **Historical periods/crises NOT named events**:
  - **global financial crisis** (lower-case — EHR is explicit)
  - **middle ages** (lower-case — EHR is explicit)
  - **old/new poor law** (lower-case — EHR is explicit)

## 2.10 Hyphens

- **Adjectival compound** (neither word an adverb): hyphenate.
  - *working-class housing* ✓
  - *nineteenth-century urbanization* ✓
  - *short-term change* ✓
- **When first word is an adverb**: NO hyphen.
  - *very rapid municipalization*
  - *newly independent country*
- **Use sparingly** in general.
- **No hyphen** on: *prewar*, *interwar*, *postwar*, *macroeconomic*.
- **Co-prefix**: *cooperate*, *coordinate*, *co-exist*.
- **Inter-prefix**: *interact* (no hyphen), but *inter-relate* (hyphen).

## 2.11 Abbreviations

- **Well-known abbreviations** should be used:
  - *Prof.* (Professor), *Co.* (Company), *Ltd.* (Limited).
- **Full stop** after abbreviated word, EXCEPT where in very common use:
  - *jr.*, *ed.* — take full stop.
  - *Dr*, *Mr*, *Mrs* — NO full stop.
- **Firm names**: use *&*, not *and* (e.g. *Smith & Co.*).
- **No full stops** in sequences of capitals: *GDP*, *OLS* — NOT *G.D.P.*
- **No full stops** in acronyms: *UNESCO*, *NAFTA* — NOT *U.N.E.S.C.O.*
- **Number**: *no.*, not *nr.*
- **Footnote abbreviations**: *pt.*, *app.*, *ch.*, *fig.*, *tab.*, *col.*
- **Cross-referencing in footnotes**: use *above* and *below* — NOT
  *supra* and *infra*.
- **UK counties**: abbreviations from appendix V of *The Concise Oxford
  Dictionary*.
- **US states**: official (NOT official postal) abbreviations.

## 2.12 Countries

- Use *United States*, not *America(n)*, where ambiguity is possible.
- Be accurate and specific with *England*, *Great Britain*, *the United
  Kingdom*.
- Be accurate with *Holland*. Avoid *the British Isles*.
- Be accurate with *European Union* (and *EU-12*, *EU-15*, etc.),
  *European Community*, *European Economic Community*.

## 2.13 Singular and plural

- *data* is a PLURAL noun.
- *media* is a PLURAL noun.
- *government*, *Parliament*, and any named company are SINGULAR.
- EHR's example: *The media are correct in saying that British Airways
  has announced record profits.*

## 2.14 Possessives

- Omit the final *s* after the apostrophe if the last syllable is
  pronounced *iz*:
  - *Bridges'*, *Moses'*
  - But: *James's*.

## 2.15 Academic titles

- Avoid including academic titles in references to individual scholars.
- The surname alone normally suffices.
